Introduction to Study in Fashion Portfolio Checklist

Portfolio Requirements:

Rotation 1: 4-8 pages for each unit

Rotation 2: 4-8 pages for each unit (Design, Business, and Media)

Basic Portfolio Requirements

  • Introduction Page: Have you included a title page that introduces your theme and entices your audience?

    Introduction Page Tips:

    • Use a compelling title that reflects your concept

    • Include a brief statement about your theme/inspiration

    • Add a striking visual element that represents your project

    • Keep text minimal but impactful

  • Research Range: Have you included both primary and secondary research?

    Research Tips:

    Primary Research (your own):

    • Your own sketches, photographs, observations

    • Interviews or surveys you've conducted

    • Museum/gallery/store visits with your photos

    Secondary Research (others' work):

    • Magazine/website images (with references)

    • Designer collections

    • Historical research

    • Trend forecasting information

  • Creative Text Use: Have you used fonts thoughtfully and creatively?

    How to Download Fonts from DaFont.com:

    1. Visit dafont.com

    2. Search for fonts that match your theme

    3. Click "Download" button next to your chosen font

    4. Extract the ZIP file to your computer

    5. For Windows: Right-click the font file → Install

    6. For Mac: Double-click the font file → "Install Font"

    7. The font will now appear in your design software

    Note: Choose 2-3 fonts maximum for consistency

  • Source References: Have you properly referenced all your sources?

    Referencing Tips:

    • Include direct references under images

    • Format: Photographer/Designer (Year) Title. [Online]. Available at: URL (Accessed: date)

    • Example: Smith, J. (2024) Spring Collection. [Online]. Available at: www.example.com (Accessed: 14 July 2025)

    • For print sources: Author (Year) Title. Publisher, Place of publication, p.##

  • Research-Ideas Connection: Have you shown clear links between your research and your own visual ideas?

    Connecting Research to Ideas:

    • Show your research inspiration alongside your designs

    • Use annotations to explain influences

    • Create side-by-side comparisons

    • Use arrows or highlighting to show specific elements you've drawn from

    • Create mood boards that visually link to your designs

  • Final Product Page: Have you included a page showing your final lineup of ideas?

    Final Product Page Tips:

    • Present your best designs/ideas together as a collection

    • Keep the layout clean and professional

    • Include brief annotations if needed

    • Show a cohesive theme connecting all pieces

    • Consider showing front/back views of designs

  • Subheadings: Have you included subheadings to articulate different aspects of your in-depth research?

    Effective Subheading Examples:

    • "Historical Influences: 1920s Art Deco"

    • "Color Analysis: Urban Landscapes"

    • "Fabric Exploration: Sustainable Materials"

    • "Target Market Research"

    • "Silhouette Development"

    • "Textile Manipulation Techniques"

  • Identification: Have you included a page with your name and ID number?

    Identification Page Tips:

    • Place at the beginning or end of your portfolio

    • Include full name, ID number, and course name

    • Keep design consistent with your portfolio theme

    • Can be combined with your title page if appropriate

  • Unit Labels: Have you included pages stating whether content is for Media, Design, or Business units?

    Unit Label Tips:

    • Create clear section dividers

    • Label each section (Design/Business/Media)

    • Use consistent formatting for all unit labels

    • Consider color-coding different units

  • Bibliography: Have you included a bibliography with Harvard referencing?

    Harvard Referencing Format:

    For Books:
    Author, A. (Year) Title. Place of publication: Publisher.

    For Journal Articles:
    Author, A. (Year) 'Title of article', Journal Title, Volume(Issue), page range.

    For Websites:
    Author, A. (Year) Title of webpage. Available at: URL (Accessed: date).

    For Images:
    Creator (Year) Title [Medium]. Available at: URL (Accessed: date).

Design Unit Checklist

  • Design Inspiration: Have you included a variety of design inspiration sources with proper image references?

    Design Inspiration Tips:

    • Include designer collections that relate to your theme

    • Show a range of historical and contemporary influences

    • Reference each image using Harvard style under the image

    • Add brief notes explaining what aspects inspire you

  • Design Development: Have you shown progression of your ideas with sketches, iterations, and refinements?

    Design Development Tips:

    • Show initial rough sketches

    • Include development drawings

    • Demonstrate how ideas evolved

    • Include both 2D drawings and 3D experiments if possible

    • Use arrows or numbering to show progression

  • Technical Elements: Have you included relevant technical details (fabric swatches, construction notes, etc.)?

    Technical Elements Tips:

    • Include fabric swatches or images if available

    • Add construction/technique notes

    • Consider including technical drawings

    • Note specific design details (collars, pockets, etc.)

    • Reference relevant manufacturing processes

  • Final Line-up: Have you created a cohesive final collection or design lineup?

    Final Line-up Tips:

    • Present your best designs as a collection

    • Ensure visual cohesion across designs

    • Show front/back views if relevant

    • Add brief annotations explaining key features

    • Keep presentation clean and professional

Business Unit Checklist

  • Market Research: Have you included research on target market, competitors, and industry trends?

    Market Research Tips:

    • Define your target customer (age, demographics, lifestyle)

    • Analyze competitor brands/products

    • Include relevant market statistics

    • Research current and upcoming trends

    • Use visuals like charts or customer profiles

  • Brand Development: Have you explored branding concepts including name, logo, and identity?

    Brand Development Tips:

    • Include brand name options and selection process

    • Show logo design development

    • Define brand values and mission

    • Create a brand mood board

    • Show packaging or merchandising concepts

  • Business Strategy: Have you outlined basic business model, pricing strategy, or marketing approach?

    Business Strategy Tips:

    • Outline your basic business model

    • Discuss pricing strategy with justification

    • Include marketing and promotion ideas

    • Consider distribution channels

    • Reference relevant business examples

  • Sustainability Considerations: Have you addressed ethical or sustainable business practices?

    Sustainability Tips:

    • Research sustainable materials or processes

    • Consider ethical manufacturing approaches

    • Explore circular economy concepts

    • Reference brands with good sustainability practices

    • Address how your business approach considers sustainability

Media Unit Checklist

  • Visual Communication: Have you effectively communicated ideas through visual media?

    Visual Communication Tips:

    • Use layout design principles (hierarchy, balance, etc.)

    • Ensure text and images work together cohesively

    • Consider how color and typography support your message

    • Create visual flow to guide the viewer

    • Reference effective visual communication examples

  • Photography/Illustration: Have you included original photography or illustrations?

    Photography/Illustration Tips:

    • Include your own photography or illustrations

    • Show development of visual concepts

    • Consider composition, lighting, and color

    • Experiment with different techniques

    • Explain your creative choices

  • Digital Media: Have you used digital tools and techniques appropriately?

    Digital Media Tips:

    • Use appropriate software for your project (Photoshop, Illustrator, etc.)

    • Show development of digital work

    • Include digital mockups if relevant

    • Consider how digital media enhances your project

    • Reference digital techniques used by professionals

  • Media Application: Have you shown how media would be applied in fashion contexts?

    Media Application Tips:

    • Show how your media would be used (campaigns, lookbooks, social media)

    • Create mockups of applications

    • Consider how your media connects to your design/business work

    • Research examples of effective fashion media

    • Include multiple formats if relevant (print, digital, etc.)

🎉 Great job! You've completed the Media Unit checklist! 🎉
